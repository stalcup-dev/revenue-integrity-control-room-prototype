# 7-Minute Reviewer Demo Script

## Framing

Target runtime: 5-7 minutes.

Open by saying:

"This is a deterministic facility-side outpatient revenue integrity control room. I'm going to show the control core first, then thin follow-through, what-if, downstream evidence, and the reviewer-ready memo layer."

## 1. Control Room Summary

- Page to open: `Control Room Summary`
- What to point at: `Open actionable exceptions`, `Recoverable dollars open`, `Already lost`, `Why The Backlog Exists`, `Where Work Is Stuck Now`, `Who Should Act Next`
- One key sentence to say: "This is the operating heartbeat of the product: current failed controls, current owner queues, and recoverable-versus-lost exposure in the same view."
- One likely reviewer objection: "This still looks like a dashboard instead of an operating queue."
- One grounded response: "The summary is only the entry point; the same slice carries current queue, current blocker, owner, SLA, recoverability, and stage aging, then the selected-case evidence trace shows exactly why one blocker owns the account now."

## 2. Queue governance / one-current-blocker / aging / ownership

- Page to open: `Opportunity & Action Tracker`
- What to point at: `Queue Priority Ranking`, then one `Selected Case Evidence Trace` with `Case Header`, `Classification`, `Queue Governance`, and `Routing History`
- One key sentence to say: "Every active item is anchored to one current blocker, one current queue, one accountable owner, and stage-specific aging instead of a pile of overlapping issue tags."
- One likely reviewer objection: "How do I know ownership and aging are not just display labels?"
- One grounded response: "The queue-governance surface shows the entry rule, exit rule, aging basis, SLA thresholds, and escalation ownership for the selected case, and queue-history proof shows believable reroutes instead of a fake one-row path."

## 3. Opportunity & Action Tracker follow-through

- Page to open: `Opportunity & Action Tracker`
- What to point at: `Intervention Snapshot`, `Recurring Issue Patterns`, `Intervention Owners`, `Intervention Plan`, and the selected intervention follow-through panel
- One key sentence to say: "This page turns exception ranking into operational follow-through by showing intervention owner, baseline metric, current metric, downstream outcome signal, and hold/expand/revise recommendation."
- One likely reviewer objection: "Is this just static project-management text?"
- One grounded response: "The current follow-through proof shows a real queue item with monitored turnaround change, an observed downstream signal, and a recommendation that is not always positive; hold, revise, and expand all exist."

## 4. Scenario Lab v0

- Page to open: `Scenario Lab`
- What to point at: the three levers, `Projected Impact`, `Scenario Output Detail`, and `How this is calculated`
- One key sentence to say: "Scenario Lab stays in-bounds by using explicit operational levers, visible formulas, and caps rather than black-box forecasting."
- One likely reviewer objection: "Scenario work often becomes fake finance math."
- One grounded response: "Here the levers are prebill edit clearance, correction turnaround, and routing speed to owner teams; the baseline sources, formulas, and caps are shown directly, and the audit note states this is what-if only, not forecast certainty."

## 5. Denial Feedback + CDM Governance Monitor

- Page to open: `Denial Feedback + CDM Governance Monitor`
- What to point at: denial pattern table, selected-pattern linkage, and the CDM governance section
- One key sentence to say: "Denials stay downstream and thin here; the point is to validate or surface upstream control failures without turning the product into a denials platform."
- One likely reviewer objection: "Why include denials at all if this is not a denials product?"
- One grounded response: "Because a thin downstream signal helps validate whether upstream workflow, documentation, or configuration failures are surfacing later, but the page explicitly stops short of appeals workflow, routing changes, or predictive denial modeling."

## 6. Revenue Integrity Decision Pack trigger

- Page to open: `Control Room Summary`
- What to point at: `Revenue Integrity Decision Pack` trigger and the rendered memo sections
- One key sentence to say: "This packages the current governed app state into a short leadership-ready artifact without inventing a separate reporting logic path."
- One likely reviewer objection: "Is this narrative layer hand-wavy or AI-generated?"
- One grounded response: "No; the audit note shows the memo is assembled from current control-room, action-tracker, scenario, KPI, and manifest outputs, and the guardrails stay explicit: facility-side, outpatient-first, deterministic-first, and what-if only."

## Close

End by saying:

"If you believe the control core, the rest of the product follows: the queues are owner-routed, the cases are traceable, the follow-through is visible, the scenario levers are bounded, and the downstream and memo layers stay thin instead of taking over the product center."
