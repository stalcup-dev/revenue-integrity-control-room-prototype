This note restores the public proof path for Scenario Lab. The goal is to show the page as a thin deterministic what-if support surface, not as a predictive product center.

## What this proves

- Scenario Lab is implemented as a current surface, not a placeholder.
- The page uses visible operational levers rather than hidden model outputs.
- The what-if framing stays bounded to the current governed slice and should be read as capped scenario math, not forecasting.
- The surface remains secondary to the deterministic control-room core.

## What this does not claim

- No predictive triage
- No optimization engine
- No enterprise financial forecast
- No replacement for the live queue, case proof, or current realism evidence
